What Are The Types Of Attention In Psychology

Attention in psychology is defined as a process through which we select certain things or stimuli from our environment and focus on them. Our attention has a limited capacity to receive stimuli. the number of objects one can attend to at a brief exposure is called span of attention or perceptual span. Different types of attention are studied in psychology and they have the following properties:

  • Concentration: is focusing on specific things or objects while excluding others for a moment.
  • Alertness: refers to the ‘readiness of an individual’ to deal with the stimuli that appear before him/her.  
  • Search: in search we look for a specific thing, object or person among a group of things, objects or people. For example when you go to buy apple in a market, you look for one specific thing in a whole array of things.

Types Of Attention In psychology

Following are the three main types of attention studied in psychology:

  1. Selective attention:
  2. Sustained attention
  3. Divided attention

Selective Attention

Our attention has a limited capacity to receive and process information at one time. Therefore, at one point we attend to only few stimuli present in our environment. That’s where selective attention comes into picture. Selective attention is mainly concerned with selecting a limited number of stimuli from a large number of stimuli preset in our environment. These limited numbers of stimuli are then processed in our system.

Factors Influencing Selective Attention

There are several factors which determine the selection of stimuli, these are as follows: external and internal factors.

  • External factors: These refer to the features of stimuli, such as the size, intensity and motion. Researchers have show that large, bright and moving stimuli easily catch our attention than small, dark and static stimuli. Similarly human photographs are more likely to get more attention than photographs of inanimate things.
  • Internal factors: are those that lie within the person and can be of two types cognitive and motivational. Cognitive factors include attitude, interest and preparatory set. Stimuli that appear as interesting are readily attended. Similarly preparedness leads an individual to readily attend to one kind of stimuli and not to others.

Motivation factors relate to our biological or social needs. For example when we are hungry we notice even the faint smell of food.

Sustained Attention

Sustained attention or vigilance refers to our ability of maintain attention on a particular stimuli for longer duration. In other words, sustained attention is mainly concerned with concentration.

Factors Influencing Sustained Attention

Studies have found several factors that facilitate or inhibit sustained attention. They are as follows:

  • Sensory modality: A sensory modality is an aspect of a stimulus or what is perceived after a stimulus. The basic sensory modalities include: light, sound, taste, temperature, pressure, and smell. Studies have shown that auditory stimuli facilitate sustained attention better than visual stimuli.
  • Clarity of stimuli: Clarity of stimuli is another factor for sustained attention. Stimuli which are intense and long lasting facilitate sustained attention and result in better performance.
  • Temporal uncertainty: When stimuli appear at regular intervals they are attended well than when they occur at irregular intervals.
  • Spatial uncertainty: Spatial uncertainty is the lack of knowledge about an object’s geographic position. Stimuli that appear at fixed places are attended better than the ones that appear at random locations.

Divided Attention

Divided attention is the ability of our brain to attend to two different stimuli from the environment at the same time. This attention is really important as it helps us to be more efficient in our daily lives. You must have seen examples of divided attention everyday in your lives, such as people driving a car and talking to a friend on phone or putting on sunglasses or listening to music.  However, divided attention comes with its own limitations; it’s possible only with activities one has practice for. In divided attention people allocate more attention to some activities than to other activities even though some attention is given to other activities.

Divided attention takes place only with highly practiced tasks, because they become almost automatic and require less attention to perform than new or slightly practiced task.

Automatic processing has three main characteristics:

  • It occurs without intention
  • It takes place unconsciously
  • It involves little or no thought processes

Conclusion

The definition of attention is psychology addresses that attention has several practical implications. The number of objects one can give attention to in a single glance is used to design the number of plates of motorbikes and cars so that the traffic police can easily notice them in case of traffic rules violation. Also, a number of children fail in school because of the problem of attention, this disorder is called as AD/HD (Attention deficient hyperactivity disorder). Difficulty in sustaining attention is the central feature of this disorder and is more prevalent in boys than in girls.
